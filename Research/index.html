Union Commitment and Its 4 Dimensions

   


 
 
      Union commitment is made up of four dimensions delineated by Gordon and colleagues (1980). The four dimensions are: Loyalty, Willingness to work for the union, Responsibility for the union, and Belief in unionism. The four dimensions are moderately to strongly correlated with each other and are indicative of the latent Union Commitment construct. Other factors from demographic variables, work-related variables, union experience variables and work-climate variables, are somewhat related to union commitment, but the relationships are rather unclear due to inconsistent results from previous studies. Based on the relationships between union commitment and other variables, several models have been designed to reveal what might promote the union commitment. So far, the integrative model from Bamberger, Kluger, and Suchard (1999) is the best fit. The model took organizational commitment, job satisfaction, pro-union attitudes, and union instrumentality perceptions as indicators of union commitment which all directly affect union commitment. Job satisfaction and union instrumentality perceptions are considered as mediators in the model.
      To better understand what factors may increase members’ commitment thus incent the member’s participation and ultimately make the union more effective, a more rigorous study should be done to combine all the inconsistent and uncertainty to yield a more reliable result.
      In this study, we used meta-analysis methods in R to analyze the relationship between union commitment’s 4 dimensions and other factors, such as demographic variables, union experience variables and work-related variables.

Gender Disparity in Published Economic Papers

   


(Picture by Solomon, C. (n.d.).)
 
       Gender disparity in economic papers has existed in the field for a long time. As more women professionals entered the study of economy, the topic and wordings of academic papers may changed. In this research project, we are interested in how female scholars’ topics and wordings differed from their male conterparts in economy research from 1970s. Would specific topic be more appealing to women than men? Would women have more voice when the research group is dominated by men?

       In this project, we used bag of words feature from natural language processing to analyze the wordings and topics from both female and male authors.

How Vaccines Help Protect Our Body From Next Attack:
Visualizing Data in Circos Plot

          


 
 
       In this study, i analyzed how differnt cells contribute to disease pathology in patients after vaccinations. I used R cloud studio and Python Pandas to analyze the data-sets. The Circos plot above was a demo from the research. In the outher layer of the graph, each section with differnt color indicated data collected from differnt vacination times in one patient. In the inner layer of the graph, the darker the shade of the green grows, the larger the weight of certain cell becomes. The grey inner line indicated the cells growth tendency over differnt time periods.
